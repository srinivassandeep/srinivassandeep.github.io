About

Academic Profile & Research Philosophy

I am a doctoral candidate in Information Systems and Operations Management at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, specializing in the strategic implications of digital platforms within food systems and sustainability frameworks.

My research program investigates the transformative effects of technology-mediated platforms on traditional market structures, with particular emphasis on food delivery ecosystems, restaurant industry dynamics, and sustainability outcomes. Through rigorous empirical analysis, I examine how digital intermediaries reshape competitive landscapes and influence social welfare outcomes, including food security and waste reduction.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I accumulated substantial industry experience in financial services and data analytics, serving in progressively responsible roles at leading institutions including Yes Bank, Axis Bank, and Reliance Industries. This professional foundation in quantitative analysis and risk assessment provides valuable practical insights that inform my academic research methodology and theoretical contributions.

My scholarly work contributes to the intersection of information systems, operations management, and platform economics, employing sophisticated econometric techniques to address complex questions of market efficiency, technological adoption, and societal impact in the digital economy.
